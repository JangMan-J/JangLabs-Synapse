## The milestone's defining decision (Phase 7 inverted the plan)

v1.1 was specced around a **scalar collision threshold** (block when projected distinct-collision
count > N). The Phase 7 shadow-calibration real-demonstration gate ran the projection over the
live corpus and the distribution came back degenerate-bimodal: `distinct_count = [0×9, 48]` —
9 trigger-bearing memories collide with nothing, 1 floods 48-way on a single broad **path** axis.
Every scalar `block≥N` (3..48) false-denies that one legitimate memory; `≥49` is inert. **No safe
scalar threshold exists.** The gate did its job: it stopped a threshold from shipping by assertion.

The plan was re-specced around a **per-component structural verdict** — BLOCK only pure-command-breadth
with no live author levers, GUIDE broad author-controlled axes at/above the single config floor
`collisionGuideFloor`, PASS otherwise (ADR-0017). A second live-corpus check before merge caught a
signal inversion in the first cut ("dead lever = sum(per_trigger)==0" — but a uniquely-routing lever
is the *best* narrowing, not a dead one); the verdict now reads `live_levers` (ADR-0019). Both
corrections were caught on the **live** corpus, not just fixtures — the v1.1 #1 rule (never false-deny
a legitimate memory at the sole fail-closed boundary) held.
